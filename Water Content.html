Water Content of Soil by Oven Drying Method

Aim

To determine natural water content of soil sample by oven drying method as per IS 2720 (Part 2)

Theory

Water content, also known as moisture content, is defined as the ratio of the mass of water present in a soil sample to the mass of dry soil solids, expressed as a percentage. It is one of the most important index properties of soil and significantly influences its engineering behavior, including strength, compressibility, permeability, and compaction characteristics.

Apparatus

  • Thermostatically controlled oven maintained at a temperature of 110°C ± 5°C.
  • Weighing balance with an accuracy of 0.04% of the mass of the soil sample taken.
  • Desiccator containing a suitable desiccating agent.
  • Airtight container made of non-corrodible material with a lid.
  • Tongs for handling hot containers.

    • Procedure

  • Clean the container, dry it, and weigh it with the lid. Record the mass as M₁.
  • Place the required quantity of the wet soil specimen in the container and close it with the lid. Determine and record the combined mass as M₂.
  • Remove the lid and place the container with the soil sample in a drying oven. Dry the sample until a constant mass is obtained, typically for 24 hours.
  • After drying, remove the container from the oven using tongs. Replace the lid immediately and allow the container to cool in a desiccator.
  • Weigh the container with the lid and the dry soil sample. Record the mass as M₃.

    Water Content Determination

    Water Content Determination

    The water content of a soil sample is determined by following these steps:

    1. Weigh the mass of the moist soil sample.
    2. Dry the sample in an oven for 24 hours at 105-110°C.
    3. Weigh the mass of the dry sample.
    4. Calculate the water content using the formula:

    Water Content (W) = (Mass of Water / Mass of Dry Soil) × 100
